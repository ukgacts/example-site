What is Web Stories By Google?

Since Google announced the arrival of web stories in google search, a lot of opportunities were opened up to them.

Many famous brands are using and experiemnting with this new format at the moment: Washington Post, BBS, CNN, Mashable, People, Guardian.

It is not a coincidence that the first adapters of web stories are big media companies. Web stories can revolutionize the way news and journalism can be presented online.

In today's fast-paced digital landscape, attention spans are shorter than ever and Web Stories content is designed to capture the viewer's attention quickly with short and easy-to-digest information.

Instead of simply listing facts web stories allow to integrate information naturally. However, the main thing about web stories is that they can play a leading role in SEO. Unlike closed ecosystems or platforms web stories appear on search engines and and can be found anywhere on the Internet.

Web stories occupy a dedicated block, called Carousel on Google Discover (SERP). They can also be referenced directly in google image. It is a gigantic traffic that opens up to them. And this demonstrates Google's desire to widely distribute this format.

Currently, over 2,000 websites have published their web stories that have been indexed by Google.

What is the technology behind web stories?

The technology of web stories is supported by Google and is based on the AMP Framework. AMP technology has gained its fame as a solution that allows you to make websites as fast as possible. Search engines reward fast sites. The faster the site, the higher its ranking on Google. Google search results contain much more than just a list of links. They are made from different panels (SERP): snippets, carousels, knowledge graphs, cards etc.

Whenever mobile search results include AMP pages, Google starts loading resources for these pages before you visit them and by the time you choose a page to visit, if this page is AMP, then it's already loaded and shows instantly. But if the page is non-AMP, then it starts to load only when you visit it. As you can imagine, there is a striking speed difference between AMP and non-AMP pages.As a result, the web pages that get most clicks from SERP panels, particularly news Panels are AMP pages.

Being a part of AMP technology web stories can also be pre-cached and therefore due to their speed prioritized by Google.

The key differences between web stories and social media stories

  • 1 ) Web stories appear directly on Google search results. They can be indexed and can be found. While social media stories can only be available to the users of the network that allow them to create stories.
  • 2) There is no expiration date for web stories. Only the owners of web stories decide how long they will last and be stored. Instagram, for example, has a limit: the duration of stories is 60 seconds and their lifespan is only 24 hours.
  • 3) Links and CTA buttons can be used in web stories. On Instagram, including links is only available to the users who have more than ten thousand subscribers.
  • 4) Unlike social media stories, when you include ads in your Web Stories, 100% of ads revenue goes to you.

How does AMP work?

There are currently three approaches to work with the AMP technology:

  • 1) Automatic Approach
    Many website builders offer automatic AMP conversions to their clients. The disadvantage of this approach is that, automatic conversion in order to improve SEO performance can easily break things. For example, a photo gallery might not work correctly or the AMP version of a page might not have a comments section.
  • 2) Coding AMP components
    The easiest way to code all AMP features correctly is to use the AMP component catalog. From small things to complex ones like web stories, AMP built-in components are the building blocks for AMP pages. The page can be made entirely from the components or only partially. To strengthen the core content pages particularly important for SEO are often duplicated in AMP. So, the search traffic becomes particularly relevant to the best that the site has to offer. This technique can be classified as a best practice.
